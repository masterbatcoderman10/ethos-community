# Product

## Register

brand (primary: landing page, campaign surfaces) / product (secondary: supporter dashboard, beneficiary profiles)

## Users

**Diaspora supporters**: Sudanese diaspora in GCC (Saudi, Qatar, UAE), UK, Europe, and USA. Sophisticated donors accustomed to development-finance standards — they expect traceability, verified identities, and auditable impact data. They are not casual charity givers; they are purposeful contributors who need to defend their giving to family, employers, or boards.

**Beneficiaries**: Displaced Sudanese families, students (especially girls), women professionals in transition, and SME operators relocated out of Sudan. They interact through verified profiles and milestone updates, not directly through the marketing surface.

**Partners**: Clinics, universities, Takaful operators, advisory firms, legal counsel — on-the-ground operators who deliver services. Secondary audience for platform credibility signals.

## Product Purpose

Ethos Community™ (Kushian™ Sudan Pilot) is a Connection-as-a-Service (CaaS) platform that links diaspora capital and expertise to displaced Sudanese communities through six partner-delivered service verticals: Education & CPD, Healthcare & Takaful, Family Support, Women Empowerment, SME Recovery, and Legal Services. Success means traceable, verified pledges that convert to measurable outcomes — not just donations, but documented lives changed.

## Brand Personality

Dignified · Traceable · Warm

Voice: editorial seriousness with human warmth. Speaks like a rigorous development-finance report that also remembers it's writing about people. No sentimentality, no pity-framing — but not cold either. Evidence-backed confidence.

## Anti-references

- GoFundMe-style pity-framing: large crying photos, donation thermometers, emotional manipulation over evidence
- Generic NGO/charity aesthetic: stock photos of smiling children, vague "we're making a difference" copy, safe blue-green palette
- SaaS startup clichés: hero metric grids, gradient text, "AI-powered" buzzwords, glassmorphism as decoration
- Government/institutional stiffness: bureaucratic language, zero emotional resonance

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over sentiment.** Impact claims carry numbers. Beneficiary stories are specific, not archetypal. The platform earns trust through data, not pathos.
2. **Dignity in every frame.** Beneficiaries are named, documented, and portrayed as agents — not victims. Design never exploits their displacement.
3. **Traceability as design language.** The audit trail (verified badges, case numbers, milestone updates) is a feature to surface, not hide.
4. **The style is already the answer.** The existing visual system — cream paper, forest green, gold, Newsreader serif — is the baseline. Future work refines and extends it, never replaces it.
5. **Diaspora-grade credibility.** This platform must withstand scrutiny from donors who can read a balance sheet. Every design decision should reinforce institutional trust, not undermine it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Audience spans multiple geographies and devices — mobile-first for diaspora users accessing on phones in GCC/UK. Arabic-language support may be needed in future; keep layout assumptions LTR-flexible. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` given the scroll-reveal animations already in the codebase.
